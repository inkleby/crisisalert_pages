THE BISHOP OF CARLISLE - July 9, 1868

Sir, the House has assembled at a Solemn Crisis, when, in the words of the Royal Speech,— 64 "The ravages of the war may be renewed, after but a few days more, unless moderation and forethought, prevailing over impediments, shall sway the councils of both the parties, whose well-being is so vitally concerned".

Address To Her Majesty On Her Most Gracious Speech.

MR. MORLEY - Feb. 9, 1871

I say it creates a most Solemn Crisis, and that hon. and right hon. Gentlemen opposite will not be able to wash themselves free from the great responsibility they have undertaken in the matter.

Pay.—(Royal Flying Corps).

Mr. LOUGH - March 24, 1914
